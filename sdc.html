What is the Self-Driving Car Challenge?

The Self-Driving Car (SDC) challenge is a collegiate engineering competition where teams design and build an autonomous vehicle capable of navigating a course without human input. Teams are responsible for the full stack — hardware, sensing, perception, and control algorithms.

It's one of the most interdisciplinary challenges in engineering: combining mechanical design, embedded systems, computer vision, machine learning, and real-time control. Students who work on SDC gain skills that are directly applicable to careers in robotics, aerospace, automotive engineering, and AI.

UB ASME will be launching this team next semester. If you're interested in being a founding member of the SDC team, reach out to us through the contact page.

What You'll Work On

Embedded Systems

Design the onboard computing architecture that processes sensor data and controls the vehicle in real time.

Computer Vision

Implement perception algorithms that let the vehicle detect lanes, obstacles, and navigate its environment.

Mechanical Design

Build and integrate the physical vehicle — chassis, motor systems, steering, and sensor mounts.

Control Algorithms

Program the decision-making systems that translate sensor input into smooth, autonomous vehicle control.
